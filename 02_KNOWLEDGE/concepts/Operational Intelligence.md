# Operational Intelligence

## Definition

The capacity of an organization to execute real work through networks of governed, reliable intelligent [[Agent|agents]]. Operational intelligence is not a product or a technology — it is an organisational capability that emerges when [[Agent Systems]], Authority Architecture]], and [[Memory Architecture]] operate together within a coherent governance framework.

## Boundaries

Operational intelligence is distinct from artificial intelligence in the model sense. Model intelligence is a component; operational intelligence is the organizational result. An organisation with powerful models but no authority architecture or structured memory does not possess operational intelligence — it possesses unstructured automation.

## Purpose

Operational intelligence is the outcome Agent Maestro exists to produce. Per [[Agent Maestro — North Star]], the goal is not smarter models but more reliable systems operating inside real organizations. The measure of operational intelligence is not model capability but [[Operational Outcome|operational outcomes]]: measurable, attributable, auditable results.

## Key Components

- [[Agent Systems]] — the execution layer: agents, workflows, and orchestration
- Authority Architecture]] — the governance layer: packs, decision authority, execution authority
- [[Memory Architecture]] — the knowledge layer: structured memory, semantic retrieval, context packs
- [[Operational Outcome]] — the evidence layer: measurable results that feed back into system improvement

## Notes

Operational intelligence requires all three layers to function. Intelligence without governance is fragile. Governance without intelligence is inert. Memory without either is dead storage. The three layers are co-dependent by design.
